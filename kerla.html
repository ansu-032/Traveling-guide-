Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is a tropical paradise known for its serene backwaters, palm-fringed beaches, and lush green landscapes. It offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and tranquility.

Top Attractions

  • Alleppey Backwaters: Explore the picturesque backwaters of Alleppey on a houseboat cruise, surrounded by coconut groves and paddy fields.
  • Kovalam Beach: Relax on the beautiful beaches of Kovalam, known for their pristine golden sands and clear blue waters.
  • Munnar: Visit the hill station of Munnar, renowned for its tea plantations, rolling hills, and breathtaking views.
  • Kochi: Immerse yourself in the rich history and culture of Kochi, a city known for its Chinese fishing nets, colonial architecture, and spice markets.
  • Periyar National Park: Embark on a wildlife safari in Periyar National Park, home to diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, and rare bird species.

Local Cuisine

Kerala is famous for its delicious and flavorful cuisine. Don't miss these traditional dishes:

  • Appam with Stew: A popular breakfast dish consisting of fermented rice pancakes served with a vegetable or meat stew.
  • Malabar Parotta with Chicken Curry: Flaky, layered flatbread served with a spicy and aromatic chicken curry.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice flour cylinders accompanied by a flavorful chickpea curry.
  • Kerala Fish Curry: A tangy and spicy fish curry prepared with coconut milk, tamarind, and a blend of aromatic spices.
  • Payasam: A traditional Kerala dessert made with rice, milk, jaggery, and flavored with cardamom and nuts.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Local Festivals

Kerala is known for its vibrant festivals, including:

  • Onam: A harvest festival celebrated with grand festivities, including traditional music, dance performances, and boat races (usually in August or September).
  • Thrissur Pooram: A magnificent temple festival featuring a procession of decorated elephants, percussion ensembles, and fireworks (usually in April or May).
  • Vallam Kali (Boat Race): Spectacular boat races held during the harvest festival of Onam, showcasing long snake boats rowed by teams of oarsmen.
